Technical Preperation

The purpose of technical interview questions is to assess if your level of technical knowledge is on par with what is required for the position you are interviewing for.

Depending on the company, technical interviews can take place over the phone, via video conferencing online and in person. The interviewing process may be an hour-long or consist of several interview rounds that can take up an entire day. During these time-frames, you can expect to face everything from regular technical proficiency questions to brainteasers and problem-solving questions.

In these interviews, you will have to prove that you have the necessary skills and abilities to handle the job responsibilities and meet the company's expectations.

Aptitude Preperation

Aptitude tests are designed to assess an individual's potential to perform a certain type of job or task. These tests typically measure cognitive abilities, such as numerical reasoning, verbal comprehension, and spatial awareness, as well as personality traits, such as emotional stability, conscientiousness, and openness to new experiences.

Aptitude tests are commonly used by employers to screen job candidates and determine their suitability for a particular role. They can also be used by individuals to assess their own strengths and weaknesses and identify potential areas for development.

Aptitude tests are usually administered online or in person and can take anywhere from a few minutes to several hours to complete, depending on the type and complexity of the test. Results are typically provided in the form of a report or score, which can be used to inform hiring decisions or personal development plans.
